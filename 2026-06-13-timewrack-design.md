# TIMEWRACK - Game Design Document
*Version 1.0 - June 2026*

---

## Logline

A cashier shot dead in a robbery wakes up as a child in a fever-nightmare world where every human era collides - medieval, modern, futuristic - and monsters walk alongside men with guns. You are the only soul in existence who can absorb the strength of what you kill. Your goal is not escape, not salvation. It is dominance.

---

## Genre & Tone

- **Genre:** Isekai-origin Souls-like Roguelite Open World
- **Tone:** Fever nightmare - surreal, dark, brutal, serious. Wrong scale, impossible architecture, dreamlike lighting. Oppressive but playable. Think Bloodborne meets a fever dream that somehow has a motorcycle in it.
- **Closest references:** Elden Ring (combat weight, world mystery), Hades (roguelite persistence), AC: Black Flag (fluid counter-based combat), Tensura/That Time I Got Reincarnated as a Slime (absorption mechanic, isekai origin)

---

## The World - The Wrack

The Wrack is a planet-sized civilization that evolved too fast. Every human era - stone age, medieval, industrial, digital, futuristic - collapsed into a single simultaneous explosion of progress. The result is a world of profound contradiction:

- Castles stand next to unfinished skyscrapers
- Knights in plate armor ride motorcycles through cobblestone streets
- Crossbows and assault rifles are sold at the same market stall
- Dragons nest inside nuclear testing ruins
- Magic and technology evolved together, tangled and indistinguishable

The civilization is advanced, literate, politically complex. Human nature never changed. There is greed, war, slavery, love, art, and religion. The technology changed. The people didn't.

### Era Zones
The open world is divided into regions where one era dominates:

- **Medieval Zones** - castles, knights, siege weapons, feudal politics, wyrms
- **Modern Zones** - urban ruins, soldiers, firearms, vehicles, industrial monsters
- **Futuristic Zones** - energy weapons, mechanized beasts, unstable physics, collapsed megastructures
- **The Deep Wrack** - uncharted territory where all eras bleed into total chaos; the further you go, the less the rules apply

### Wrack Beasts
Creatures born from the collision of eras. Nature mutated alongside technology:
- Dragons with tank armor plating fused into their scales
- Wolves the size of houses with exposed ribcage machinery
- Knights whose armor grew into their bodies centuries ago
- Soldiers who have been alive so long they have become something else

## The Story

### Who You Are
You were a cashier. You were shot dead during a convenience store robbery - a cop's stray bullet, wrong place, wrong time. The most undignified death imaginable.

You wake up as a **6-year-old child** in a dense alien forest with no idea where you are, only the faint ghost of your previous life pressing against the back of your skull.

### Act 1 - Childhood (1–1.5 hours)
A second-year Academy student named **Sera** finds you during a field expedition at the forest's edge. She doesn't know what you are. She asks no complicated questions. She simply brings you in.

The childhood phase is about discovery. You cannot fight. You explore the Academy's grounds, ask questions, learn the world through Sera's patient answers and your own small observations. Basic movement, basic interaction, no combat. The world is enormous and strange and you are very small.

Sera is not your mentor. She is not chosen. She is just kind. That matters.

### Act 2 - Academy Years (1.5–2 hours)
You enroll as a student. Teen years. You study combat theory, world history, monster classification, alchemy, engineering. You attend classes, form relationships, discover your abilities quietly and privately.

Combat is introduced here - sparring, controlled exercises, low-stakes encounters outside the walls. You begin to notice you learn faster than anyone else. You say nothing.

Graduation is the threshold. The story truly begins when you walk out of the Academy gates as an adult and the open world unlocks.

### Act 3 - The Open World (3+ hours, infinitely replayable)
No more structure. No more classes. The Wrack in full - era zones, Wrack Beasts, factions, named enemies, bosses, the Deep Wrack. The roguelite loop activates. Die, lose current gear and position, return to a Bonefire anchor with persistent upgrades, run again.

Your goal: become the apex predator of The Wrack. Not a hero. Not a savior. The last one standing.

### The Archivist
In the Academy's oldest tower lives a figure known only as **The Archivist** - ancient, genderless, who has catalogued every soul to ever arrive in The Wrack. They noticed you the moment you woke up in the forest.

In their ledger, beside your name, they wrote three words:

> *"Wrack of Wracks."*

They will not approach you for years. They are waiting to see if the ledger is right. When they finally speak to you, it will change everything you think you know about why you're here.

## Core Gameplay Systems

### Combat - The Black Flag Model
Fluid, rhythmic, counter-based combat in the style of AC: Black Flag. You feel like a predator when you master it; getting swarmed punishes greed. Melee and firearms coexist naturally - draw a pistol mid-combo, finish with a zweihander, sheathe and roll.

Key principles:
- **Counter windows** - well-timed counters chain into devastating combos
- **Gear weight matters** - plate armor slows you, leather lets you flow
- **Era mixing has real mechanics** - plate armor stops blades well but bullets poorly; riot shields deflect crossbow bolts; a zweihander with momentum staggers Kevlar-wearing soldiers
- **No stamina bar** - punishment comes from positioning and greed, not a meter

### The Era Chaos System - Random Loot Per Run
Every run randomizes your starting era loadout. You never know if you'll find a zweihander or an AK first. The loot pool spans all eras:

- **Medieval:** zweihanders, tower shields, plate armor, crossbows, war axes, helms
- **Early Modern:** flintlock pistols, cannons, leather coats, pikes, rapiers
- **WW-era:** bolt-action rifles, gas masks, trench coats, bayonets, grenades, field armor
- **Modern:** ARs, body armor, riot shields, flashbangs, combat knives, vehicles
- **Hybrid Abominations:** chainmail with ballistic plate inserts, shields with mounted pistols, halberds with bayonets, dragon-scale riot gear

Hybrid items are rarer and more powerful - they represent The Wrack's civilization at its most chaotic.

### Absorption - Why You Are the Wrack of Wracks
You are the only soul in The Wrack who can absorb the power of what you kill. No one else can do this. No one knows why.

- **Common enemies** - killing gives passive stat boosts (strength, speed, resilience, perception). Incremental. Stackable. Every kill makes you marginally harder.
- **Named enemies** - unique individuals with histories and skills. Killing one gives you a specific active ability drawn from their life. A veteran knight's perfect guard-break. A sniper's breath-hold technique. A gang leader's intimidation aura.
- **Bosses** - the true prizes. Boss absorptions are transformative. Kill a dragon and absorb something ancient and terrible that no Academy discipline can teach. These are the abilities that make you inhuman.

Absorption is automatic on kill. The weight of what you've consumed is visible - over time, your character begins to move differently, carry themselves differently. The cashier is still in there. But so is everything else.

### Roguelite Persistence - Echo Shards & Echo Orbs
On death:
- Lose current gear and position
- Keep **Echo Shards** and **Echo Orbs** earned during the run
- Spend Echo Shards at Bonefires to permanently expand the loot pool, unlock new era zones, or deepen the hybrid item table

The more you unlock, the weirder and more chaotic each run becomes. The reward loop deepens the madness.

Absorbed abilities from named enemies and bosses **persist across deaths** - they are part of you now, not gear. Stat boosts from common kills reset. This creates a meaningful asymmetry: the cashier's body resets, but what the Wrack of Wracks has consumed does not.

### The Echo Anchor System
Echo Orbs can be spent to **anchor** any piece of gear to your soul - a weapon, armor piece, shield, anything. Anchored items survive death and wait for you at your Bonefire at the start of the next run.

- **Anchor slots start at 1** - one precious item persists. Early runs force an agonizing choice: your best weapon or your best armor?
- **Slots unlock with Echo Orbs** - spend orbs to permanently add more anchor slots, gradually building a signature loadout that defines your identity across runs
- **Any gear can be anchored** - weapons, armor, shields, hybrid abominations, all eligible
- **Anchoring costs scale** - anchoring a common sword is cheap; anchoring a dragon-scale riot shield or a legendary hybrid weapon costs significantly more orbs

The anchor system creates a natural progression arc. Early runs feel raw and desperate - one anchored item, everything else lost. Late runs feel like a predator returning home - your signature gear waiting, absorbed abilities intact, the world slightly more afraid of you than last time.

## Key Characters

### Sera
Second-year Academy student when she finds you. Warm, curious, unremarkable in the world's eyes. She becomes a recurring figure throughout the Academy years - a friend, a constant, someone who knew you when you were small and lost. She has no destiny. She is just good.

### The Archivist
Ancient. Genderless. Has catalogued every soul in The Wrack for longer than the Academy has existed. Knows what you are before you do. Will not interfere until the moment is right. Morally ambiguous - their interest in you is genuine but their agenda is their own.

## What Makes TIMEWRACK Fresh

No game combines:
- Isekai origin story with genuine emotional slow-burn (childhood → academy → open world)
- Souls-weight combat with AC: Black Flag's fluid counter system
- True roguelite open world with persistent ability absorption
- Random cross-era loot chaos as the core identity
- A protagonist whose power fantasy is rooted in petty, human, completely understandable spite

The closest neighbors are Elden Ring, Hades, Noita, and every isekai anime ever made. None of them put a cashier in plate armor holding a shotgun and a dragon's heartbeat.
